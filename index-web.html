🛠️ MANUAL BACKUP MODE — Injury tier reference
Injury Filter — Tier Reference
Rule: Injuries that affect offensive production get filtered. Defensive injuries have minimal impact. Use game-day injury reports 2 hours before kickoff.
TIER 1: No Adjustment (0)

Minimal impact on scoring. No confidence adjustment needed.

Examples:

  • Defensive linemen (DL) out
  • Linebackers (LB) out (except elite coverage guys)
  • Backup cornerbacks (CB) or safeties (S)
  • Deep bench skill players / practice squad
  • Special teams contributors
  • Kickers / Punters
  • Long snapper / holder
  • 3rd/4th string RB or WR
TIER 2: Role Player Out (−1)

Moderate effect on production. Reduces confidence by −1.

Examples:

  • Starting cornerback (CB) or safety (S)
  • Pass rush specialist / EDGE rusher (non-elite)
  • Backup QB (starter is healthy)
  • #2 or #3 WR (depth chart)
  • #2 RB (handcuff back)
  • Starting TE (if backup is adequate/proven)
  • Guard or Center (O-line depth)
  • Coverage linebacker (not all-pro)
  • Slot cornerback (role-specific)
TIER 2.5: Starting Skill Player Out (−1.5)

High effect on production. Reduces confidence by −1.5.

OFFENSIVE

  • Starting WR1 or WR2 (both key targets)
  • Starting RB1 (elite/feature back)
  • Starting TE (elite production/target share)
  • Left tackle (franchise LT, pass protection)
  • Left guard (elite pass blocker)

DEFENSIVE

  • Star cornerback (elite coverage)
  • All-Pro pass rusher (EDGE)
  • Coverage safety / ball hawk
  • Run defense anchor (DT)
  • All-pro linebacker (rare)
TIER 3: Elite Player Out (DROP)

⚠️ AUTO-DROP THE PICK. DO NOT PLAY.

Game-changing injury. Model uncertainty too high.

ALWAYS DROP

  • Starting QB (any starter out)
  • RB1 (elite feature back)
  • WR1 (top target, 20%+ share)
  • Franchise LT (pass protection anchor)
  • TE1 (elite production back)

LIKELY DROP

  • All-Pro cornerstone player
  • Top 5 draft pick player
  • Star defender (playoff impact)
  • Consensus All-Pro (any position)
  • Player with 15%+ target/snap share

Rationale: When elite players go out, backup replacements have historically underperformed model expectations. Too many variables to predict accurately. Better to skip than guess.

Status Classifications: Check final 2-hour pregame report. Injuries shift between "Out," "Questionable," and "Probable" — use the LATEST status before kickoff. Use official NFL injury reports, ESPN, team beat reporters, and team official announcements.
⚠️ EDGE CASE: If a backup has significant playing time this season (8+ starts), reduce the filter tier by 1 (Tier 2.5 → Tier 2). Proven backups hurt less than untested rookies.
