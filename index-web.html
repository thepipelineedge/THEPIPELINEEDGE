🛠️ MANUAL BACKUP MODE — Injury tier reference
Injury Filter — Tier Reference
Rule: Injuries that affect offensive production get filtered. Defensive injuries have minimal impact. Use game-day injury reports 2 hours before kickoff.
TIER 1: No Adjustment (0)

Examples:

  • Defensive linemen (DL)
  • Linebackers (LB) out
  • Backup cornerbacks (CB)
  • Deep bench skill players
  • Special teams contributors
  • Kickers/Punters

Impact: Minimal effect on scoring. No confidence adjustment needed.

TIER 2: Role Player Out (−1)

Examples:

  • Starting cornerback (CB)
  • Starting safety (S)
  • Pass rush specialist (EDGE)
  • Backup QB (starter healthy)
  • #3 WR or #2 RB
  • Starting TE (if backup adequate)

Impact: Moderate effect. Reduces confidence by −1

TIER 2.5: Starting Skill Player Out (−1.5)

Examples:

OFFENSIVE

  • Starting WR1 or WR2
  • Starting RB1 (elite)
  • Starting TE (elite)
  • LT/LG (pass protection)

DEFENSIVE

  • Star cornerback (elite)
  • All-Pro pass rusher
  • Coverage safety

Impact: High effect. Reduces confidence by −1.5

TIER 3: Elite Player Out (DROP)

⚠️ Auto-DROP the pick. Do not play.

Examples:

ALWAYS DROP

  • QB1 out (any starter)
  • RB1 (elite) out
  • WR1 (elite) out
  • LT (franchise)

LIKELY DROP

  • Star defender (All-Pro)
  • Top 5 draft pick player
  • Franchise cornerstone

Impact: Game-changing. Model uncertainty too high. DROP PICK.

Check official NFL injury reports (12 hours before game), ESPN injury reports, and team beat reporters for final status. Classifications change between "Out," "Questionable," and "Probable" — use final 2-hour pregame report.
